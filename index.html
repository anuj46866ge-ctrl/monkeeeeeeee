Your Soul is Pure

Your soul is pure like a diamond,
So brave and so strong, no matter what lies ahead.
Your heart is warm and endlessly precious,
Loving others more than yourself instead.

Your eyes are deeper than the ocean blue,
And when I look into them, I drown —
Afraid I may never come back up for air,
Yet secretly hoping I never do.

Your lips are soft like forbidden fruit,
A sweetness I may never taste,
But even the thought of them
Makes my heart race.

Your smile shines brighter than galaxies,
More beautiful than the Seven Wonders could ever be.
And every time I look at you, I wonder —
Are you real, or just a dream meant for me?

If this is a dream, please don’t wake me up,
It’s the most beautiful one I’ve ever known.

Your skin glows like fireflies in the night,
Brighter than a full moon softly shone.
I hope I see you soon —
because every day, that’s the quiet wish on my mind.

Talking to you makes time slow down,
As if the world gently fades away.
To me, you are a crown —
Rare, powerful, profound.

A single day with you eases my pain,
Makes my heartbeat soar like an airplane.
Like an espresso shot on a winter morning,
You awaken my soul again and again.

The more I have of you,
The more I crave —
Not out of need,
But because your presence feels like home.

And there is something I try to say,
Every time we meet.
I rehearse it quietly in my mind
On my way to see you.
I promise myself,
“This time, I won’t hold back.”

But then I look at you —
And suddenly,
The simplest words become the hardest to speak.

Not because they are wrong.
Not because they are uncertain.
But because your presence
Has a way of humbling me.

So I hide it behind small smiles
And unfinished sentences,
Hoping one day
I’ll be brave enough
To say out loud
What my heart has known all along.
